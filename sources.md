## Candidate sources

1. [Personalized Quest and Dialogue Generation in Role-Playing Games: A Knowledge Graph- and Language Model-based Approach](https://consensus.app/papers/details/0551b1b0b6b45be7b6deec007ad77ed7/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Trevor Ashby, Braden K. Webb, Gregory Knapp, Jackson Searle, and Nancy Fulda, 2023, *Proceedings of the 2023 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems*, citation count: 67.  
   Relevance: Useful because it studies quest and dialogue generation for RPGs and emphasizes grounding generated content in game context.

2. [Generating Role-Playing Game Quests With GPT Language Models](https://consensus.app/papers/details/8fe8aef4fcf155e8b719d5a26178040c/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Susanna Vartinen, Perttu Hamalainen, and C. Guckelsberger, 2024, *IEEE Transactions on Games*, citation count: 66.  
   Relevance: Very close to the project. The abstract reports that generated quest descriptions vary widely in quality, which supports a practical evaluation approach.

3. [Procedural Content Generation in Games: A Survey with Insights on Emerging LLM Integration](https://consensus.app/papers/details/57dd23d56b32552dbbef0283f265b18f/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Mahdi Farrokhi Maleki and Richard Zhao, 2024, venue not listed in Consensus output, citation count: 19.  
   Relevance: Useful background on how LLMs fit into the longer history of procedural content generation.

4. [Expanding Expressive Range: Evaluation Methodologies for Procedural Content Generation](https://consensus.app/papers/details/61b9fd4b5a795b699677585dbca22b1f/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   A. Summerville, 2018, venue not listed in Consensus output, citation count: 51.  
   Relevance: Useful for the method section. It supports the idea that evaluating generators needs more than cherry-picked examples.

5. [RPGBENCH: Evaluating Large Language Models as Role-Playing Game Engines](https://consensus.app/papers/details/935969f15c0b5cdb8fe733c0aeae1699/?utm_source=chatgpt)  
   Pengfei Yu et al., 2025, *arXiv*, citation count: 6.  
   Relevance: Useful as a modern source direction for testing whether LLMs can balance creativity, coherence, and mechanics in RPG-style systems.

## How these sources would support the paper

- Sources 1 and 2 establish that LLM-based quest generation is a real research topic.
- Source 3 gives the broader procedural content generation background.
- Sources 4 and 5 support the evaluation angle: the important question is not just "can it generate text?" but "does the generated content satisfy game-design constraints?"

## Citation caution

Do not cite these sources as proof that a small classroom Space generates production-ready quests. A safer claim is: "Research on procedural quest generation suggests that fluency and usefulness must be evaluated separately."
